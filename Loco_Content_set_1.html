Locomotor and Nonlocomotor Unit

Content Set I

  • EQ1: How do I keep balance when jumping galloping, jogging, sliding, and skippi
  • EQ2: What pathways can I move in?
  • EQ3: What shapes can I make with my body?
  • EQ4: What are my body parts?
  • EQ5: What is the difference between stretching my body and curling my body?
  • EQ6: What is the difference between personal and general space?
  • EQ7: What do I need to do to change speeds when moving?
  • EQ8: Why is it important to follow directions?

    • Prerequisite skills/movement concepts necessary for success:

    • Concepts – NA
    • Skills
      1. Walking
      2. Crawling
      3. Creeping
    • Skills introduced/refined in unit
      1. Hop
      2. Jump
      3. Gallop
      4. Side
      5. Jog/run
      6. Bending
      7. Stretching

    Unit Movement Concept Focus:

    • Space
      1. Personal and General Space
      2. Pathways (Curved, Straight, Zig-Zag)
      3. Directions
      4. Range
      5. Levels
      6. Speed
    • Body Awareness
      1. Identification of body parts
      2. Locomotor and Non-Locomotor skills
        1. Force
    • Alignment with Locomotor/Non-Locomotor/Manipulative Skills/Rhythmic Activities/Developmental Gymnastics

    Equipment:
    • Hula Hoops (1 per student)
    • Long Ropes (1 per student or 1 rope per 2-3 students)
    • 2-3 sets of various height hurdles (6 inch, 8 inch, 12 inch, 18 inch)
    • 10-12 Small Cones
    • 4-8 Large Cones
    • 15-20 Poly Spots
    • Equipment to make obstacles
    Teaching Materials/Resources:
    • Music appropriate to grade level/activity (see resources)
    • Speakers (Bluetooth or other)
    • Cues posters
    • Skills posters
    Facilities/Space: Gymnasium
    Time: 40 minutes
    Teacher Expertise/Training/Certification:
    • Knowledge of motor development
    • Ability to identify and demonstrate fundamental locomotor and non-locomotor skills
    • Understanding of movement concepts and relationship to performance of locomotor and non-locomotor skills
    • Knowledge of developmentally appropriate activities and games.

    Lesson Outcomes/Objectives
    1. Students will stretch to make their bodies as tall as possible when directed by the teacher (S1 – 1.24)
    2. Students will explore personal space within a hoop by making their bodies as flat as possible (S1 – 1.2.4)
    3. Students will demonstrate a change in levels while bending and stretching (S1 – 1.2.4)
    4. Students will correctly indicate their head, knees, shoulders, and toes during the “Heads, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes” activity (NA)
    5. Students will follow the prompts of the “Freeze Song” activity (S3 – 3.2.4)
    Equipment
    • Hula Hoops (1 per student)
    Teaching Resources
    • “Freeze Song” – Mr. Heath
    • “Head, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes” Song
    Lesson Activities
    Lesson activities for Day 1 including time, activity, description, and diagram
    Time Activity Description Diagram
    5 min Instant Activity: “Freeze Song” (Mr. Heath Album: Seriously Silly iTunes)
    • Students are instructed to enter gym and find a hoop and stand in that hoop
    • If 2 students go to the same hoop – both of you should find a new hoop
    • If you are having trouble finding a hoop – you can come to the “Help Station” – which is me!
    • Students each have a hula-hoop, that is placed on the floor.
    • Students will stand in the hoop (representing self-space) and follow directions of the song.
    • The song uses the cue “Freeze” for students to stop the previous action.
    • “Unfreeze” is the cue for students to resume moving.
    		Content Set I: Day One Diagram
    Transition
    • At completion of Instant Activity – on “move signal” students will leave hoops on the floor and walk to circle in center of the gymnasium (spots could be used as well)
    • Students sit on lines of circle or spots
    Set Induction (Introduction to Lesson)
    • Students are sitting in ½ or ¾ circle
    • Set Induction:
      • Quick reminder – what do you do when you hear me (or the song) say “Freeze”?
      • Today we are going to learn about some of our major body parts: our Head, Shoulders, Knees and Toes and what we can do with them:
        • Stretch – make our body as tall as possible
        • Bend
        • Make our body look like a pancake (flat)
        • What level are and what it means to change levels
    • The first thing we are going to do is listen to a song and do what the song says – do the best that you can.
    Transition
    • On my signal – “GO” students will get up and stand in a hoop
    • If 2 students go to the same hoop – both of you should find a new hoop
    • If you are having trouble finding a hoop – you can come to the “Help Station” – which is me!
    • Walking – “Go”
    10 min Pre-Assessment:
    Body Parts: “Head, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes” - iTunes     		“Head, Shoulders, Knees, [&] Toes”
    • Students stand in Hula Hoop
    • Students will stay in hoop while following directions of song and touching their heads, shoulder, knees, and toes.
    • The song helps students learn body parts and practice changing levels while listening to and following movement cues.
    Pre-Assessment: using the recording form, teacher indicates when a student cannot correctly touch one of the body parts identified in the song.
    Transition
    • At completion of Pre-Assessment – and on “Freeze” signal – students stop and wait for further instructions.
    • On the “Go” signal – students are asked to sit in their hoops to listen for the instructions for the first learning activity
    Skill Concept Development (Learning Activity Progression)
    20 min Introduction to:
    • Personal Space
    • Bending and Stretching
    • Body Parts: Heads, Shoulders, Knees, Toes
    		All students are standing in hoops spread out in general space. Music can be used as stop/start signals. Teach:
    1. Difference between personal and general space
    2. Exploration of personal space (hoop)
      1. Hold arms out straight to side and in front
        1. Can you touch anyone else?
        2. How close are you to someone else?
      2. Fill the hoop with your body
    3. Exploration of personal space and range
      1. Tall – Small
      2. Wide – Flat
    Introduce Bending and Stretching while in Personal Space (students in hoops)
    1. Teacher Demonstration of skills (students repeat)
      1. Stretch – full extension of body – arms extended above head
      2. Bend
        1. At elbow
        2. At knee
        3. At the waist
    2. On signal – students follow prompts such as:
      1. Make yourself as tall as you can
      2. Make yourself as flat and wide as you can
      3. Bend body part
    3. Introduction to Body Parts (students in hoops)
      1. Mimic teacher touching and naming body parts: Head – Shoulders – Knees – Toes
      2. Mimic teacher touching and naming body parts: Elbows – Ankles – Hips – Wrists
    		Content Set I: Day One Diagram
    Transition
    • On “Freeze” signal – stop and stand in hoop.
    • Listen to directions for “Head, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes”
    Application Activity/Assessment
    10 min “Head, Shoulder, Knees & Toes” Song Activity/Assessment “Head, Shoulders, Knees, [&] Toes”
    • Students stand in Hula Hoop
    • Students will stay in hoop while following directions of song and touching their heads, shoulder, knees, and toes.
    Assessment: using the recording form, teacher indicates when a student cannot correctly touch one of the body parts identified in the song. Compare results from Pre-assessments     		Content Set I: Day One Diagram
    Transition
    • On signal – students bring hula hoops to designated area
    • Walk to circle in center of the gymnasium (spots could be used as well)
    • Students sit on lines of circle or spots for Closure
    Closure Check for Understanding Questions:
    • Can you touch your head?, your shoulders? Your knees?, your toes?
    • Can everyone show me how to check for your personal space? (reach arms out)
    • Who can bend their elbow? Your knee? Your waist?
    • Who can show me a High level?
    • Who can show me a low level?
    Next time we will practice our Head, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes and learn a few more body parts and ways our body can move.

    Lesson Outcomes/Objectives
    1. Students will correctly indicate their head, knees, shoulders, toes, ankles, hips, wrists, and elbows during the modified “Heads, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes” activity (S1 – 1.2.4)
    2. Students will be able to demonstrate swinging and swaying during the modified “Head, Shoulders, Knees , and Toes…..” activity. (S1 – 1.2.4)
    3. Students will be able to point to their hip, ankles, wrist and elbows during the modified “Head, Shoulders, Knees , and Toes…..” activity. (S1 – 1.2.4)

    Lesson Activities
    Lesson activities for Day 2 including time, activity, description, and diagram
    Time Activity Description Diagram
    5 min Instant Activity: “Heads, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes” Activity “Head, Shoulders, Knees, & Toes”
    • Students stand in Hula Hoop
    • Students will stay in hoop while following directions of song and touching their heads, shoulder, knees, and toes.
    		Content Set I: Day Two Diagram
    Transition
    • At completion of Instant Activity – on “move signal” students will leave hoops on the floor and walk to circle in center of the gymnasium (spots could be used as well)
    • Students sit on lines of circle or spots
    Skill Concept Development (Learning Actvity Progression)
    15 mins Set Induction (Introduction to Lesson)
    • Last class we learned about Personal Space. What does that mean to you?
      • Possible Answers
        • “my own bubble”
        • “my special space”
        • “a place that is mine”
        • “no one else is allowed into my space without an invitation”
    • Today we will review Personal Space, Body Shapes, Bending and Stretching and body parts we learning in the song Head, Shoulders, Knees and Toes.
    • We are also going to learn about more body parts and swinging and swaying
    		Content Set I: Day Two Diagram
    Transition
    • On my signal – “GO” students will get up and return to the hoop that they were in during Instant Activity (walking)
    • Stand in hoop and wait for teaching instructions for Review Activity
    15 min Review:
    1. Explore Personal Space within Hoop
    2. Review Body Shapes (Tall, Small, Flat, Wide)
    3. Review Bending and Stretching
    4. Review Activity: “Bend and Stretch – Make yourself Small and Tall”
    5. Body Parts: Review Head, Shoulder, Knees, and Toes
    1. Explore Personal Space within Hoop upon teacher direction – reminding students to stay within imaginary “walls of hoop”. Prompts: “Make yourself”…. (teacher makes note of students who may not understand prompts – Formative Assessment)
      1. As tall as you can
      2. As small as you can
      3. As wide as you can
      4. As round as you can
    2. Review of Body Shapes: Tall; Small; Flat; Wide; Round (Cues)
      1. Teacher demonstration and use of cues (and/or use of video, posters, etc.)
      2. Students replicate teacher action within hoop
    3. Review Bending and Stretching (Cues)
      1. Teacher demonstration and use of cues (and/or use of video, posters, etc.)
      2. Students replicate teacher action within hoop
    4. Review Activity: “Bend and Stretch – Make yourself small and tall” (See developmental activity/game attachment)
    5. Review Activity: “Head, Shoulder, Knees, and Toes” (Repeat Instant Activity)
    Transition
    • At completion review activity – and on “Freeze” signal – students stop and wait for further instructions.
    • On the “Go” signal – students are asked to sit in their hoops to listen for the instructions for the first learning activity
    10 min Introduce Wrist, Elbow, Hip, Ankle:
    1. Teacher Directed
    2. Review Body Shapes (Tall, Small, Flat, Wide)
    3. Student call of body part: head, shoulders, knees, toes, wrist, elbow, hip, ankle
    		Students remain standing in hoops
    1. Teacher directed –
      1. Teacher identifies and points to body part
      2. Shows actions
      3. Students mimic actions
    2. Student Call – students in hoops
      1. Teacher selects student to call body part and perform actions
      2. All students follow
      3. Teacher selects new student to call a new body part
    10 min Swinging and Swaying:
    1. Swinging Arms – Teacher Demonstration/Student Practice – in Hoop (Back and forth; Up and down (flapping))
    2. Body Swaying – Teacher Demonstration/Student Practice – in Hoop
    3. Swing and Sway
    1. Swinging Arms
      1. Back and forth
        1. Teacher demonstration
        2. Students repeat (standing in hoops)
        3. Cues
      2. Up and down (Flapping)
        1. Teacher demonstration
        2. Students repeat (standing in hoops)
        3. Cues
    2. Body Swaying
      1. Teacher demonstration
      2. Students repeat (standing in hoops)
      3. Cues
    3. “Swing and Sway” – (See developmental activity/games)
    Application Activity/Assessment
    5 min Assessment Task: Modified “Heads, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes – Swing and Sway” Activity (including elbow, wrist, hips, ankles) Students will stay in hoop while following directions of song and follow actions of teacher (use instrumental version of song):

    • Walk to circle in center of the gymnasium (spots could be used as well)

    Closure Check for Understanding Questions:

    Next time we will practice our Head, Shoulders, Knees, Toes, Ankles, Hips, Wrist and Elbow with Swinging and Swaying. We will also learn about different shapes we can make with our bodies and pathways we can chose when moving and the difference between walking and jogging.
