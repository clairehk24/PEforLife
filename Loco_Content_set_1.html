  • EQ2: How can learning to play basketball help you play other invasion games?
  • EQ3: Why is force production and force absorption important when playing basketball?
  • EQ4: How can basketball help individuals learn to work as a team?
  • EQ5: Why are rules necessary to play basketball safely and fairly?
  • EQ6: How can playing basketball contribute to a person’s overall fitness both now and in the future?
  • EQ7: How do the different ways of scoring affect game play during basketball?
  • EQ8: How can a team most effectively keep the other team from scoring?
  • EQ9: How can fair play enhance the enjoyment of the game of basketball?

    Equipment:
    1. Hoops: Two hoops with the rim at 10 feet from the ground are needed to play a full court basketball game. Games can be modified to play half-court games depending on the class size and gym/outdoor space.
    2. Basketballs: High school students should use basketballs that are 28.5-29.5 inches in circumference (size 6 or size 7).
    3. Courts: Indoor or outdoor courts can be used for this unit. Indoor courts are encouraged as the floors are typically safer to prevent injuries, but outdoor courts can be used or introduced so students can witness that basketball can be played indoors and outdoors.
    4. Jerseys: It’s not required but it’s encouraged that teams use some sort of jersey or pinnie to designate teams. Basketball is such a fast-paced sport and it makes it difficult to locate team members and opponents without jerseys.
    5. 5. Scoreboards: Electronic or manual scoreboards are encouraged. Students will be able to keep track of the score while competing. Students will also be able to assist with scorekeeping while their team is completing other tasks for basketball other than playing.
    6. Other: Cones, tennis balls, buckets, paper, pencils, poster board, markers/colored pencils, music (optional)
    Facilities/Space: Indoor or outdoor courts can be used for this unit. Indoor courts are encouraged as the floors are typically safer to prevent injuries, but outdoor courts can be used, so students can witness that basketball can be played indoors and outdoors. For full court regulation games, two hoops with the rim at 10 feet from the ground are needed. Games can be modified to play half-court depending on the class size and gym/outdoor space.
