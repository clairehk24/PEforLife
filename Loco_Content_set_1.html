Locomotor and Nonlocomotor Unit

Content Set I

  • EQ1: How do I keep balance when jumping galloping, jogging, sliding, and skipping?
  • EQ2: What pathways can I move in?
  • EQ3: What shapes can I make with my body?
  • EQ4: What are my body parts?
  • EQ5: What is the difference between stretching my body and curling my body?
  • EQ6: What is the difference between personal and general space?
  • EQ7: What do I need to do to change speeds when moving?
  • EQ8: Why is it important to follow directions?

    • Prerequisite skills/movement concepts necessary for success:

    • Concepts – NA
    • Skills
      1. Walking
      2. Crawling
      3. Creeping
    • Skills introduced/refined in unit
      1. Hop
      2. Jump
      3. Gallop
      4. Side
      5. Jog/run
      6. Bending
      7. Stretching

    Unit Movement Concept Focus:

    • Space
      1. Personal and General Space
      2. Pathways (Curved, Straight, Zig-Zag)
      3. Directions
      4. Range
      5. Levels
      6. Speed
    • Body Awareness
      1. Identification of body parts
      2. Locomotor and Non-Locomotor skills
        1. Force
    • Alignment with Locomotor/Non-Locomotor/Manipulative Skills/Rhythmic Activities/Developmental Gymnastics

    Equipment:
    • Hula Hoops (1 per student)
    • Long Ropes (1 per student or 1 rope per 2-3 students)
    • 2-3 sets of various height hurdles (6 inch, 8 inch, 12 inch, 18 inch)
    • 10-12 Small Cones
    • 4-8 Large Cones
    • 15-20 Poly Spots
    • Equipment to make obstacles
    Teaching Materials/Resources:
    • Music appropriate to grade level/activity (see resources)
    • Speakers (Bluetooth or other)
    • Cues posters
    • Skills posters
    Facilities/Space: Gymnasium
    Time: 40 minutues
    Teacher Expertise/Training/Certification:
    • Knowledge of motor development
    • Ability to identify and demonstrate fundamental locomotor and non-locomotor skills
    • Understanding of movement concepts and relationship to performance of locomotor and non-locomotor skills
    • Knowledge of developmentally appropriate activities and games.

    Students will stretch to make their bodies as tall as possible...
    Explore personal space within a hoop.
    Follow “Freeze Song” & “Head, Shoulders, Knees, Toes”.
    Pre-assessment on identifying body parts.
    Learn and demonstrate bending/stretching.
    Closure: questions about personal space & body parts.

    Review body parts from Day 1.
    Introduce wrists, elbows, hips, ankles.
    Add movement: swinging & swaying.
    Modified “Head, Shoulders, Knees & Toes” song.
    Formative assessment built into music-based activity.
    Closure: demonstrate swinging/swaying & point to parts.

    Practice maintaining personal space in general space.
    Learn difference between walking and jogging.
    Introduce body shapes: straight, curved, zig-zag.
    Practice moving in various pathways using hoops and ropes.
    Closure: Identify shapes and describe differences between movements.

    Review arm swings for jumping.
    Practice vertical and horizontal jumps using hoops.
    Introduce “Astronauts” game to apply jumping concepts.
    Focus on landing safely and with balance.
    Closure: cues for jumping and landing.

    Review vertical/horizontal jump with “Hike in the Woods” course.
    Introduce hopping using “Stork” cue poster.
    Practice hopping in all directions: forward, back, left, right.
    Add jumping and hopping in different directions.
    Apply skills in “Animal Actions” activity (frog, bird, snake).
    Closure: body awareness and hopping review.

    Review hopping and jumping cues.
    Introduce skipping and galloping with cues/posters.
    Practice in open space and in obstacle setups.
    Use “Hike Through the Woods II” obstacle course for practice and assessment.
    Closure: demonstrate skip, gallop, and discuss how to land softly.

    Assessment using “Freeze Song” (following directions).
    Review skills with “Astronauts” warm-up.
    Perform “Hike in the Woods III” with assessment stations:
    – Hop (Lily Pads)
    – Gallop (Fields)
    – Skip (Flowers)
    Closure: demonstrate skills & reflect on progress.
