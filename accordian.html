    Lesson Outcomes/Objectives
    1. Students will stretch to make their bodies as tall as possible when directed by the teacher (S1 – 1.24)
    2. Students will explore personal space within a hoop by making their bodies as flat as possible (S1 – 1.2.4)
    3. Students will demonstrate a change in levels while bending and stretching (S1 – 1.2.4)
    4. Students will correctly indicate their head, knees, shoulders, and toes during the “Heads, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes” activity (NA)
    5. Students will follow the prompts of the “Freeze Song” activity (S3 – 3.2.4)

    Lesson Activities
    Lesson activities for Day 1 including time, activity, description, and diagram
    Time Activity Description Diagram
    5 min Instant Activity: “Freeze Song” (Mr. Heath Album: Seriously Silly iTunes)
    • Students are instructed to enter gym and find a hoop and stand in that hoop
    • If 2 students go to the same hoop – both of you should find a new hoop
    • If you are having trouble finding a hoop – you can come to the “Help Station” – which is me!
    • Students each have a hula-hoop, that is placed on the floor.
    • Students will stand in the hoop (representing self-space) and follow directions of the song.
    • The song uses the cue “Freeze” for students to stop the previous action.
    • “Unfreeze” is the cue for students to resume moving.
    		Content Set I: Day One Diagram
    Transition
    • At completion of Instant Activity – on “move signal” students will leave hoops on the floor and walk to circle in center of the gymnasium (spots could be used as well)
    • Students sit on lines of circle or spots
    Set Induction (Introduction to Lesson)
    • Students are sitting in ½ or ¾ circle
    • Set Induction:
      • Quick reminder – what do you do when you hear me (or the song) say “Freeze”?
      • Today we are going to learn about some of our major body parts: our Head, Shoulders, Knees and Toes and what we can do with them:
        • Stretch – make our body as tall as possible
        • Bend
        • Make our body look like a pancake (flat)
        • What level are and what it means to change levels
    • The first thing we are going to do is listen to a song and do what the song says – do the best that you can.
    Transition
    • On my signal – “GO” students will get up and stand in a hoop
    • If 2 students go to the same hoop – both of you should find a new hoop
    • If you are having trouble finding a hoop – you can come to the “Help Station” – which is me!
    • Walking – “Go”
    10 min Pre-Assessment:
    Body Parts: “Head, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes” - iTunes     		“Head, Shoulders, Knees, [&] Toes”
    • Students stand in Hula Hoop
    • Students will stay in hoop while following directions of song and touching their heads, shoulder, knees, and toes.
    • The song helps students learn body parts and practice changing levels while listening to and following movement cues.
    Pre-Assessment: using the recording form, teacher indicates when a student cannot correctly touch one of the body parts identified in the song.
    Transition
    • At completion of Pre-Assessment – and on “Freeze” signal – students stop and wait for further instructions.
    • On the “Go” signal – students are asked to sit in their hoops to listen for the instructions for the first learning activity
    Skill Concept Development (Learning Progression)
    20 min Introduction to:
    • Personal Space
    • Bending and Stretching
    • Body Parts: Heads, Shoulders, Knees, Toes
    		All students are standing in hoops spread out in general space. Music can be used as stop/start signals. Teach:
    1. Difference between personal and general space
    2. Exploration of personal space (hoop)
      1. Hold arms out straight to side and in front
        1. Can you touch anyone else?
        2. How close are you to someone else?
      2. Fill the hoop with your body
    3. Exploration of personal space and range
      1. Tall – Small
      2. Wide – Flat
    Introduce Bending and Stretching while in Personal Space (students in hoops)
    1. Teacher Demonstration of skills (students repeat)
      1. Stretch – full extension of body – arms extended above head
      2. Bend
        1. At elbow
        2. At knee
        3. At the waist
    2. On signal – students follow prompts such as:
      1. Make yourself as tall as you can
      2. Make yourself as flat and wide as you can
      3. Bend body part
    3. Introduction to Body Parts (students in hoops)
      1. Mimic teacher touching and naming body parts: Head – Shoulders – Knees – Toes
      2. Mimic teacher touching and naming body parts: Elbows – Ankles – Hips – Wrists
    		Content Set I: Day One Diagram
    Transition
    • On “Freeze” signal – stop and stand in hoop.
    • Listen to directions for “Head, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes”
    Application Activity/Assessment
    10 min “Head, Shoulder, Knees [&] Toes” Song Activity/Assessment “Head, Shoulders, Knees, [&] Toes”

    • Students will stay in hoop while following directions of song and touching their heads, shoulder, knees, and toes.

    Transition
    • On signal – students bring hula hoops to designated area
    • Walk to circle in center of the gymnasium (spots could be used as well)
    • Students sit on lines of circle or spots for Closure
    Closure Check for Understanding Questions:
    • Can you touch your head?, your shoulders? Your knees?, your toes?
    • Can everyone show me how to check for your personal space? (reach arms out)
    • Who can bend their elbow? Your knee? Your waist?
    • Who can show me a High level?
    • Who can show me a low level?
    Next time we will practice our Head, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes and learn a few more body parts and ways our body can move.
